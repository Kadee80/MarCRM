## Trends in the PR Freelance Market (this run)

1. **Agency overflow is concentrating in fintech/crypto.** Three of the four leads are PR agencies (Dukas Linden, M Booth, Method). Of those, Dukas Linden and Method are explicitly fintech-focused, and M Booth's open req sits in the B2B corporate-affairs band where fintech and professional-services clients dominate. The signal: independent fintech-PR demand is outpacing what permanent agency headcount can absorb, and Q2 2026 is producing a steady supply of "senior freelance VP" overflow seats.

2. **PR M&A is feeding the overflow market.** The Dukas Linden / Infinite acquisition (May 6, 2026) is the third major financial-comms agency rollup we've logged this quarter (alongside earlier Gregory FCA / BackBay integration and the Edelman Smithfield buildout). Each rollup creates a 60-90 day overflow window where freelance senior counsel can plug client gaps during integration. Mark should systematically watch financial-PR M&A announcements as a leading indicator of freelance demand.

3. **In-house "Head of Comms" searches at fintechs are interim opportunities.** Tide joins a multi-month watchlist (Tether on 2026-05-18, BitGo on 2026-05-18, Reserv on 2026-05-12) of fintechs running long-cycle Head of Communications searches. The pattern is consistent: 4-6 month searches, scarce senior fintech-PR talent, in-house team underwater. Interim coverage during the search is a defensible, repeatable pitch.

4. **Remote eligibility is widening at large agencies.** M Booth's 21-state remote eligibility (including all the major freelance-PR hubs) is meaningful. Two years ago this role would have been NYC-only. The talent market has won the work-from-anywhere argument inside corporate-affairs PR — that materially expands Mark's addressable freelance pool.

5. **Pharma freelance is heating up but is off-ICP.** Edelman, FleishmanHillard and other holding-co networks are running explicit freelance Account Director contracts for pharma/health PR (3-month, £48-72K equivalent). Worth knowing as a market datapoint but **excluded from this scrape** because Mark's ICP is finance / legal / PE/VC / B2B / asset management / fintech, not pharma.
