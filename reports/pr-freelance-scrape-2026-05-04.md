## 1. Offchain Labs — Senior Communications & PR Manager (combined 71, B)

**Company:** Offchain Labs builds the Arbitrum stack — the most widely adopted Ethereum L2 scaling solution. Backed by $124M (notably Lightspeed, Polychain, Pantera). Mature product, large ecosystem, scale-up team.

**Why it matters for Mark:** The job description says the Senior Comms & PR Manager will *"oversee external PR agencies and partners globally."* That is the single most actionable phrase in this entire scrape. Unlike most "Head of PR" roles where Mark would be pitching to a candidate-search committee, this role explicitly already routes work to outside agencies. Two doors open instead of one:

1. **The hire's first 90 days** — when a new Senior Comms Manager onboards, the existing agency roster gets re-evaluated. New leadership often wants their own freelance bench. That's a window to be on a shortlist.
2. **The interim gap** — until they hire, the existing agency carries everything. Agencies under load look for white-label/overflow help.

**Pitch angle:** Two-track. (a) LinkedIn note to Offchain Labs CEO Steven Goldfeder positioning Mark as available for fintech-adjacent / institutional Web3 narrative work as the Senior Comms Manager search runs. (b) Try to identify the incumbent PR agency (search PRNewswire and Arbitrum Foundation press releases for boilerplate) and pitch them on overflow.

**Risk:** Same as Robinhood Crypto and Coinbase Base — Web3/L2 fluency is mandatory. If Mark cannot pass a five-minute conversation about rollups, sequencers, and Arbitrum vs. Optimism, this is a NO regardless of fit. Mark should self-assess honestly before spending outreach cycles here.

## 2. GlossGenius — Director, PR & Communications (combined 68, B)

**Company:** GlossGenius is a NYC-based vertical SaaS for beauty pros (booking, payments, marketing). Bessemer-backed, growing, well-known in NYC SMB SaaS. Not Mark's sweet spot sectorally — consumer-facing SMB SaaS sits two steps away from his fintech / legal-tech / asset-management core — but the workstream is squarely B2B/enterprise PR-shaped.

**Why it matters for Mark:** This Director, PR & Communications role has been **continuously open and posted since Feb 6, 2026**. That is roughly **three months on the market**. A search this stale signals one of three things, all of which are pitchable:

1. The hiring bar is high and they have not found their candidate → real capacity gap.
2. Internal priority shifted but the job is still being maintained → narrative lead is being deferred.
3. They are getting work done by an outside agency or freelancer in the meantime → competitor exists, but pricing and scope re-evaluation likely.

In any of these scenarios, an interim 60-90 day retainer covering "narrative, exec thought leadership, and media relationships" while the search continues is well within Mark's wheelhouse — and frees up GlossGenius leadership to keep waiting for the right Director hire instead of compromising.

**Pitch angle:** Single LinkedIn note to GlossGenius CMO (whoever is the comms-function owner). Frame: *"You've had this Director search open for ~3 months. While you keep searching for the right permanent hire, I can carry the media-relations and exec thought-leadership workstreams on a 60-day interim, no long-term commitment, hand off cleanly when you find your Director."* Reasonable retainer: $10-15K/month for that scope.

**Risk:** Sector adjacency is meaningful. SMB / consumer SaaS press contacts (Allure, beauty trade) are different from B2B fintech press contacts. Mark should be honest in outreach that his Rolodex is B2B/finance-tilted, but lean on the workstream overlap (narrative architecture, exec thought leadership) which is sector-agnostic.

**Source:** https://job-boards.greenhouse.io/glossgenius
