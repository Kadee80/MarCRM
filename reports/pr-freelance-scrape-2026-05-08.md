## Trends — PR Freelance Market (Week of May 2-8, 2026)

- **Agency M&A is creating overflow windows.** Infinite/Dukas Linden is the second financial-PR consolidation in 24 months (Gregory FCA bought BackBay in 2024). Each merger opens a 6-12 week senior-freelancer demand spike during integration. Watch O'Dwyer's and PRovoke for the next deal — this is now a repeatable trigger to monitor weekly.
- **Vetted-freelancer networks are hardening as a real channel.** The Work Crowd, Toptal Communications, Mediabistro Freelance Connect, and similar networks are increasingly used by mid-size agencies (50-150 headcount) as a faster alternative to staffing-firm contractors. Worth registering on 2-3 in parallel.
- **PE-backed PR holding companies = sustained overflow demand.** ParkSouth → Infinite, Stagwell, Next 15, etc. PE comp models pressure agencies to keep variable cost (freelance) higher than full-time payroll. Mark's senior-bench profile is structurally well-positioned against this trend.
- **Boutique financial PR shops (Vested, William Mills, Caliber, BackBay/Gregory FCA, DLPR/Infinite, Cognito) remain Mark's tightest ICP cluster.** The 5/8 scrape doubles the named agency-overflow pool. Sustained outreach cadence to this list (1-2 fresh contacts per week) should produce a steady-state pipeline.
