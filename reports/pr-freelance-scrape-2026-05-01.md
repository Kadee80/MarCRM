## Trends in PR hiring market (week of 2026-04-24 → 2026-05-01)

1. **Direct freelance/contract PR postings remain rare — but B2B-tech agency freelance rosters are an underused alternative path.** Six of eight leads this week are agency-overflow / interim-during-search patterns. The two exceptions are Mission North (an actual named freelance roster that takes intake) and, partially, Siege Media (which is buying-not-renting capacity but creating a vendor relationship in the gap). Worth formalizing: "agency with named freelance roster" deserves its own signal flag in the scoring rubric and a recurring monthly scan of the top 20 B2B tech and fintech PR agencies for freelance-intake pages.

2. **Stablecoin/crypto-payments fintech is the hottest sub-sector for pr-freelance leads right now.** BVNK (this scrape), Crypto.com (last week), Robinhood Crypto (this scrape), Coinbase Base (this scrape) — four crypto/fintech-payments comms leadership searches in two weeks. The sector is in a hiring wave. Mark's positioning advantage if he is crypto-fluent is real; if he is not, the sub-sector signal is a red flag, not a green flag, because every conversation will quickly test crypto chops.

3. **First-PR-hire signal continues to be quiet at funded operating companies.** No fresh "first PR hire" or "building PR department" postings hit the threshold this week from a non-agency operating company. HockeyStack from last week's scrape (post-$50M raise, no in-house comms) remains the strongest example. The closest analogue this week is BVNK building their global PR function from scratch — but BVNK is mid-flight on the VP search, not pre-search, so the window is narrower.

4. **Combined-role searches (Policy + PR, IR + Comms, Marketing + Comms) are a hidden source of extended search windows.** Atticus (Director, Policy + PR) is this scrape's example. These combined roles take 4-6 months to fill instead of 8-12 weeks. Worth flagging combined-mandate roles as a specific subcategory in scoring — the extended search is structural opportunity, not a coincidence.

5. **Agency-overflow as a category is widening.** 5WPR last week, Siege Media and Mission North this week — that's three consecutive scrapes with agencies either hiring senior PR leadership or running active freelance rosters. Worth treating "PR agency hiring senior leadership OR with named freelance pipeline" as a recurring high-priority signal for this pipeline. Mark should keep a running short list of mid-tier B2B-friendly agencies (5WPR, Siege Media, Mission North, RH Strategic, SHIFT Communications, Avenue Z, MVPR, Black Unicorn PR, BeantownMV) and scan them monthly for both senior leadership openings AND freelance-roster intake.
