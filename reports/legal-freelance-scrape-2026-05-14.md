## Market Trends — Legal Freelance, week of 2026-05-14

- **ALSPs are the dominant channel this week.** Four of six leads route through Latitude Legal. Latitude continues to publish comp bands ($160–200k+ standard, $225–278k+ for the energy/advanced-tech M&A role) and offers W2 + full benefits at 20+ hr/wk plus complimentary PLI — a materially better contractor package than the typical 1099 fractional shop. Worth building one strong AM relationship there.
- **Commercial contracting is the deepest sub-vertical for contract work.** The volume of remote commercial/technology-contracts postings (Latitude, Viskase, ACC Jobline listings, Indeed's "Contract Counsel Remote" board) far exceeds fund-formation contract roles, which skew toward permanent or hourly-billable big-firm programs (Ropes & Gray, already in the DB).
- **Fund-side freelance demand is real but mostly arrives as perm GC searches.** Radian Capital, and the BlackRock role from the prior scrape, are FT postings — the freelance opportunity is in the interim-coverage pitch, not the listed role. Emerging-manager and family-office interim GC demand is growing per market commentary but rarely surfaces as a clean public posting.
- **Part-time + remote is no longer rare.** Three of six leads are explicitly part-time and remote (Viskase, the Latitude energy/M&A role) or fractional/interim — a continued shift from the same signal in the 05-12 scrape.
- **Fractional GC pricing benchmark holding steady.** Service-provider data this week consistently quotes $3,000–$8,000/month for fractional GC engagements (vs. $250k–$400k+ for a full-time GC) — a useful anchor for Katie's own retainer pricing conversations.
