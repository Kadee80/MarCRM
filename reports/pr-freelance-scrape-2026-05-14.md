## Trends in the PR freelance market

- **Boutique financial-comms agencies are federating, not merging.** The Haven Tower Alliance model — independent firms keeping their brands while sharing go-to-market — is spreading. For a senior freelancer this is structurally good: alliances generate cross-referral deal flow faster than member firms can hire, and they prefer trusted senior contractors over junior FTEs for client-facing overflow.
- **"Interim" is becoming a productized service line, not a stopgap.** Conway markets "interim IR support" as a named offering. Forthright Advising and The Work Crowd do the same for interim/fractional comms directors. Mark should mirror this language in outreach — "interim IR" and "fractional comms" are now buyer-recognized categories, not euphemisms.
- **Freelance is now the majority of some comms teams.** Mediabistro/Adweek reporting this cycle pegs freelancers at up to 70% of some marketing teams, with the most senior-skewed roles increasingly contract rather than FTE. The market is "rewarding specificity" — narrow, senior, well-defined skill sets win.
- **Post-funding comms demand is real but rarely posted.** Rogo, Adfin, Comfi, Sahi and Frame Security all raised in the last ~2 weeks; none posted a PR role. Funding announcements remain the best cold-approach signal — the need is created the day the round closes, months before a job listing appears.
- **Senior-only freelance pricing holds.** Boutique senior-led retainers are benchmarking at $12K–$25K/month; senior fractional comms day rates are well into four figures. The "100% of budget on senior expertise" pitch is the market-standard framing.
